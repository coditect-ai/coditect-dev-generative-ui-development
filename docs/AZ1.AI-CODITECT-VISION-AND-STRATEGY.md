## AI Risk Management Framework

### The CODITECT Approach to Responsible AI

**Multi-Dimensional Risk Assessment:**

#### 1. Technical Risk Management

**Computer Science Perspective:**
- Model validation and testing
- Bias detection and mitigation
- Performance monitoring and drift detection
- Version control and reproducibility
- Secure model deployment

**Key Questions:**
- Is the AI model performing as expected?
- Are there edge cases causing failures?
- Is the model degrading over time?
- Can we reproduce results deterministically?

#### 2. Ethical Risk Management

**Ethical Perspective:**
- Fairness and non-discrimination
- Privacy preservation
- Human autonomy and dignity
- Societal impact assessment
- Stakeholder consultation

**Key Questions:**
- Does this AI system treat all users fairly?
- Are we respecting individual privacy rights?
- Does this enhance or diminish human agency?
- What are the broader societal implications?

#### 3. Security Risk Management

**Security Perspective:**
- Data encryption (at rest, in transit, in use)
- Access controls and authentication
- Threat modeling and penetration testing
- Incident response planning
- Supply chain security

**Key Questions:**
- Is sensitive data protected from unauthorized access?
- Can the system withstand attacks?
- What happens if there's a breach?
- Are third-party dependencies secure?

#### 4. Governance Risk Management

**Governance Perspective:**
- Clear accountability structures
- Decision-making authority
- Compliance requirements
- Policy enforcement
- Oversight mechanisms

**Key Questions:**
- Who is responsible for AI decisions?
- How are conflicts resolved?
- Are we compliant with regulations?
- Who has authority to override AI decisions?

#### 5. Auditability Risk Management

**Audit Perspective:**
- Complete audit trails
- Timestamped decision logs
- Input/output tracking
- Model versioning
- Change management records

**Key Questions:**
- Can we reconstruct past AI decisions?
- Is there a complete chain of custody?
- Can external auditors verify our processes?
- Are we maintaining adequate records?

#### 6. Explainability Risk Management

**Transparency Perspective:**
- Interpretable models when possible
- Explanation generation for predictions
- Feature importance tracking
- Decision reasoning documentation
- User-friendly explanations

**Key Questions:**
- Can we explain why the AI made this decision?
- Is the explanation understandable to stakeholders?
- What factors influenced this outcome?
- Can users challenge AI decisions?

#### 7. Accountability Risk Management

**Financial & Operational Accountability:**
- Cost tracking and attribution
- ROI measurement
- Resource allocation
- Budget oversight
- Performance metrics

**Creation & Development Accountability:**
- Development lifecycle documentation
- Change request tracking
- Testing and validation records
- Deployment approval workflows
- Post-deployment monitoring

**Key Questions:**
- Who approved this AI deployment?
- What was the total cost of development?
- How do we measure success?
- What happens when AI fails?
- Who bears liability for AI errors?

## Human-in-the-Loop Decision Framework

### Workflows That Should Always Be Human-Managed

**1. Strategic Decision-Making**
- Long-term business strategy
- Major investment decisions
- Organizational restructuring
- Market positioning and pivots
- Partnership and M&A decisions

**Why:** Requires nuanced understanding of context, stakeholder relationships, and long-term consequences that AI cannot fully comprehend.

**2. Ethical and Legal Approvals**
- AI model deployment approvals
- Privacy impact assessments
- Legal contract review and signing
- Regulatory compliance attestations
- Ethical review board decisions

**Why:** Human judgment on ethical implications, legal liability, and regulatory compliance cannot be delegated to AI.

**3. Security Reviews**
- Security architecture approvals
- Incident response decisions
- Access control policy changes
- Vulnerability disclosure decisions
- Third-party security assessments

**Why:** Security decisions carry existential risk and require human accountability.

**4. Data Governance**
- Data classification decisions
- Data retention policies
- Third-party data sharing approvals
- Consent management
- Right-to-be-forgotten requests

**Why:** Legal and ethical implications of data handling require human oversight.

**5. Assumption Analysis**
- Business model assumptions
- Market size estimations
- Risk probability assessments
- Scenario planning
- Strategic forecasts

**Why:** AI can provide data-driven insights, but humans must validate assumptions based on domain expertise and intuition.

**6. Strategic Analysis of AI-Generated Work**
- Review of AI-generated code (before production)
- Validation of AI-generated reports
- Approval of AI-recommended changes
- Assessment of AI-proposed strategies
- Quality assurance of AI outputs

**Why:** Human expertise is needed to catch errors, validate reasoning, and ensure alignment with organizational goals.

**7. Sensitive Workload Identification**
- Classification of workflows as sensitive
- Determination of local vs. cloud processing
- Identification of 3rd party LLM restrictions
- Data sovereignty requirements
- Competitive intelligence protection

**Why:** Only humans can understand the full context of what makes information truly sensitive.

### Workflows Prohibited from 3rd Party / Public LLMs

**Must Use Local AI Workstations (Data Cannot Leave Enterprise):**

1. **Healthcare - Protected Health Information (PHI)**
   - Patient records analysis
   - Clinical decision support
   - Medical research with identifiable data

2. **Financial - Sensitive Financial Data**
   - Customer account information
   - Trading strategies
   - Financial forecasting with proprietary models

3. **Legal - Attorney-Client Privilege**
   - Legal strategy discussions
   - Case analysis with confidential information
   - Contract negotiations

4. **Corporate - Trade Secrets**
   - Proprietary algorithms
   - Unreleased product designs
   - Competitive intelligence
   - M&A due diligence

5. **Government - Classified Information**
   - National security matters
   - Law enforcement investigations
   - Regulatory enforcement actions

6. **HR - Employee Personal Data**
   - Performance reviews
   - Compensation analysis
   - Disciplinary actions

**CODITECT Solution:** Local AI workstations with air-gapped models, ensuring data never leaves the enterprise network.

- **Success Criteria:** 3x conversion rate in regulated industries
- **Pivot:** If false, focus on non-regulated first, build case studies

**Hypothesis 3: Workflow Analysis Drives Enterprise Adoption**
- **Test:** Free workflow analysis as lead generation
- **Success Criteria:** 40% of analyses convert to pilot
- **Pivot:** If false, shift to product-led growth model

- **Test:** Customer interviews on vendor lock-in concerns
- **Success Criteria:** >60% cite vendor flexibility as top-3 concern
- **Pivot:** If false, simplify to single-LLM initially
